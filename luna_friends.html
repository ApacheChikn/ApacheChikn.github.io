- To Some of the Most Exceptional People I Know -

An idea was born and put into motion. As soon as the date was set I was looking forward to it. My favorite group of people were going to meet in person. The idea was to spread kindness with painted rocks. How does one spread kindness with painted rocks? Well, they paint each rock with artistic love and a motivational message. Then, they spread the rocks around for people to unexpectantly find them. The person sees the unusual rock and looks closer to find a beautiful message of kindness, caring, hope, inspiration, motivation, tenderness, and love. What an awesome idea! Get together with friends — cheering each other up — to paint rocks — and spread cheer to others.

For me, the day was perfect. You see, I live a very lonely life. The loneliness is so painful I cry often thinking about how I am not worthy enough to spend time with. But this day, I was going to be with some of the most exceptional people I know.

Let's start with how I met them. One of my sisters knows the pain I live with from loneliness. She was going to Luna Mindful Infusions to receive ketamine therapy. She became part of the email list and was invited to a new online group, "Weekly Integration Circle", started by Kathleen McGuire, the Founder & CEO of Luna Mindful Infusions. My sister sent me the invitation and I attended. I enjoyed myself so much the first time I kept coming back; every Tuesday at 9 am. It was the best way to start my day — at least Tuesday. I am always excited to attend. The topics we discuss are relevant and relatable. There is empathy, compassion, and healing that takes place. I love every minute of our hour together. I feel alive. My pain disappears for one amazing hour every Tuesday.

You are appreciated, my Luna family. You made me feel visible, valued, special, and part of something bigger. For that, I am deeply grateful. You are appreciated, and I love all of you. ❤️

I thank God for the blessing bestowed upon me, that is Integration Circle.

Shawn W. Feltmate

One thing that I have learned through all of my struggles is that kindness is a healer. Whether you are being kind to someone or you are on the receiving end of kindness, it can have a great effect on your life. Trying to think of ways to spread kindness and form a tighter bond with the Luna family, the rock painting event was born! I really just wanted to spread kindness and to make it fun. These rocks will now be spread throughout the community for everyone to see. What a great way to enjoy each other's love and kindness and then send it out in the world.

Justin Odom
